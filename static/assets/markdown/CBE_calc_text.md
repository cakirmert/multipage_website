The tool calculates the sum of all cation charges (Mg2+, Ca2+, Na+, K+) and all anion charges (Cl-, SO4 2-, NO3-, plus total alkalinity).
The charge-balance error (CBE) is expressed as a signed relative percentage. Its sign shows whether cations or anions are in excess.
In aquatic geochemistry, an absolute relative CBE within +/-5% is generally considered acceptable. Values outside this range are highlighted for review.

The ions must be included with their valency, for example *2 for Ca2+ and *1 for Cl-.

The relative charge-balance error (CBE) is calculated as:

$$
\text{CBE [%]} = \frac{\sum \text{cations} - \sum \text{anions}}{\sum \text{cations} + \sum \text{anions}} \times 100
$$

where charge sums use equivalent concentrations after conversion to the selected unit basis.

The signed charge imbalance is defined as:

$$
\Delta Q = \sum \text{cations} - \sum \text{anions}
$$

The absolute imbalance is $|\Delta Q|$. The +/-5% screening threshold is applied to the absolute value of the relative CBE.

The default values represent the average seawater composition. The CBE is not exactly zero because additional minor ions contribute to the total
charge beyond the major cations and anions included here. However, a value smaller than 1% indicates very good data quality.
